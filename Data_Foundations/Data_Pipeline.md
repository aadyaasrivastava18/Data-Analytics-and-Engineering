# Data Pipeline

## What is a Data Pipeline?

A Data Pipeline is a sequence of processes that collects data from one or more source systems, transforms it according to business requirements, and delivers it to a destination such as a Data Warehouse.

---

## Why Do We Need Data Pipelines?

Without a pipeline:

- Every analyst writes different SQL.
- KPIs become inconsistent.
- Business logic gets duplicated.
- Manual effort increases.
- Leadership loses trust in the data.

With a pipeline:

- Business logic is centralised.
- Data is cleaned and validated.
- Reports remain consistent.
- Manual work is reduced.
- Analytics becomes scalable.

---

## Business Example

Google receives raw data from:

- Google Ads
- Gmail
- Maps
- YouTube
- Android

A pipeline:

1. Collects the data.
2. Cleans it.
3. Applies business rules.
4. Loads trusted data into the Data Warehouse.

---

## Interview Question

**Why are data pipelines important?**

Data pipelines ensure every analyst works with the same business logic, reducing inconsistencies and enabling trusted reporting.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Pipeline = Centralized Business Logic.
- Reduces repetitive work.
- Improves consistency.
- Makes analytics scalable.
